Grammar notes and declension tables

Verbs that take the dative

These verbs are followed by a dative object rather than an accusative:

persuadeoI persuade
resistoI resist
-io verbs (3rd conjugation)

These 3rd conjugation verbs end in -io and follow a slightly different pattern — their stem vowel is i throughout the present tense:

faciodo, make
fugioflee
capiocapture
cupiodesire
accipioreceive
iaciothrow
coniciohurl
interficiokill
3rd declension -ium words

These nouns have -ium in the genitive plural instead of the usual -um. The ablative singular is the normal -e.

urbs, urbis f.city
mors, mortis f.death
nox, noctis f.night
parens, parentis m.parent
dens, dentis m.tooth
pons, pontis m.bridge
mons, montis m.mountain
3rd declension -ium words: same nominative & genitive singular

These nouns share their nominative and genitive singular form, and take -ium in the genitive plural:

hostis, hostis m.enemy
navis, navis f.ship
classis, classis f.fleet
civis, civis m.citizen
Same nominative & genitive singular — but not -ium

These nouns also share their nominative and genitive singular, but their genitive plural ends in -um, not -ium:

canis, canis m.dog
iuvenis, iuvenis m.young man
mare, maris n. — sea

A neuter i-stem noun: ablative singular ends in -ī (not -e), and the plural uses i-stem endings throughout.

1st declension

Feminine (and a few masculine). e.g. puella, -ae f. (girl)

e.g. servus, -ī m. (slave)

No feminine nouns in the 2nd declension (except a few like humus, ground). Vocative singular ends in -e. Exception: nouns ending in -ius take vocative -i instead (e.g. filius → fili).

Neuter rule: nominative, vocative, and accusative are always identical. In the plural they end in -a.

3rd declension — masculine & feminine

e.g. rex, regis m. (king); vox, vocis f. (voice)

Masculine and feminine are largely identical. The -ium genitive plural (and -i ablative singular for i-stems) belongs to i-stem nouns: e.g. civis (citizen), hostis (enemy).

3rd declension — neuter

e.g. nomen, nominis n. (name); mare, maris n. (sea)

Nom./voc./acc. are always identical (neuter rule). I-stem neuters (e.g. mare) take -ia in the plural and -i in the ablative singular instead of -e.
